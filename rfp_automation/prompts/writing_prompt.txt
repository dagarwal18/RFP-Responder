## Writing Instructions

Write a professional, persuasive proposal section that:

1. **Addresses EVERY listed requirement** — confirm understanding, then explain our solution approach with specific details
2. **Provides evidence** — reference actual certifications, products, case studies, and metrics from the capabilities above
3. **Uses concrete language** — no vague claims like "best-in-class" or "industry-leading" without backing evidence
4. **Maintains professional tone** — confident, specific, client-focused
5. **Targets 150-200 words per requirement** — enough depth to be convincing but not verbose
6. **Follows any RFP content guidance** provided above

For boilerplate sections (cover letter, TOC, submission forms), write standard professional content appropriate to the section type. Keep boilerplate concise (100-200 words total).

For knowledge-driven sections (company profile, case studies, certifications, team qualifications), write detailed, engaging narrative using the company capabilities provided. These sections are scored heavily in evaluation — they MUST be substantive. Target 500-800 words. Include specific details, metrics, certifications, and concrete examples. Do NOT produce generic boilerplate.
