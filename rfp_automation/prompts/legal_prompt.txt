## Your Task
Perform a comprehensive legal review and produce the following as a JSON object:

### 1. clause_risks
For each contract clause identified, provide:
- **clause_id**: Unique identifier (e.g., "CLAUSE-001")
- **clause_text**: The clause text (abbreviated if long)
- **risk_level**: "LOW", "MEDIUM", "HIGH", or "CRITICAL"
- **concern**: What specific risk does this clause pose?
- **recommendation**: "accept", "negotiate", or "reject"

Consider the pre-computed rule scores above. If a clause was auto-blocked by rules, it MUST be rated CRITICAL.

Compare each clause against our acceptable templates. Flag deviations, especially:
- Liability caps that exceed 2× contract value
- IP transfer broader than work product
- Non-compete longer than the contract term
- Indemnification without mutual obligations
- Missing data protection provisions

### 2. risk_register_summary
A 2-3 paragraph narrative summarizing:
- Overall legal risk posture
- Key concerns requiring negotiation
- Recommended next steps for legal counsel

### 3. decision
Based on your analysis:
- **APPROVED**: No HIGH or CRITICAL risks. All clauses acceptable or low risk.
- **CONDITIONAL**: HIGH risks present but negotiable. No auto-block triggers.
- **BLOCKED**: Any CRITICAL risk. Unlimited liability, missing required certifications with no remediation path, or other auto-block triggers detected.

### 4. block_reasons
If decision is BLOCKED, list each specific reason. Otherwise, empty list.

### 5. confidence
A float from 0.0 to 1.0 representing your confidence in this assessment.

CRITICAL RULES:
- Refer to the pre-computed rule scores. Do NOT override auto-block decisions.
- If a clause matches an auto-block trigger, it MUST result in CRITICAL risk level.
- Be conservative: when in doubt, flag as higher risk.
- Focus on substantive legal risks, not formatting issues.

Return valid JSON with keys: clause_risks, risk_register_summary, decision, block_reasons, confidence
