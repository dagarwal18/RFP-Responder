1.1  About Apex Industrial Logistics Limited  
Apex Industrial Logistics Limited (hereinafter "Apex" or "the Company") is one of India's leading integrated 
logistics and heavy supply chain operators. Founded in 1994 and headquartered in Bhiwandi, Maharashtra, 
Apex operates a national network of three h undred (300) branch warehouses spanning twenty -eight (28) Indian 
states and Union Territories, with a centralised Tier -III Data Centre located in Andheri East, Mumbai.  
 
Apex employs approximately 15,000 personnel across its operational footprint and recorded consolidated 
annual revenue of approximately INR 5,500 Crore in Financial Year 2024 –25. The Company's core business 
verticals encompass bulk freight forwarding, autom ated warehousing, cold -chain logistics, last -mile 
distribution, and cross -border trade facilitation. Apex is a publicly listed entity on the National Stock Exchange 
(NSE: APEXIL) and is subject to SEBI listing obligations.  
1.2  Current Infrastructure Challenges and Business Drivers  
Apex's current enterprise network infrastructure was architected over a decade ago and is now a material 
constraint on operational agility, application performance, and security posture. The incumbent environment 
presents the following critical and unaccep table constraints:  
 
Pain Point  Current Impact on Operations  
Legacy MPLS Network  All 300 branch warehouses are connected via legacy Multiprotocol Label Switching 
(MPLS) circuits procured from a single incumbent carrier. Bandwidth tiers are rigid 
(2 Mbps to 10 Mbps per branch), costly to upgrade, and require 60 –90-day lead 
times for any  configuration change.  
Chronic Network 
Outages  The Company experienced an average of 4.3 unplanned network outages per 
month per region in FY2024 –25, each lasting an average of 47 minutes. These 
outages directly halt warehouse management system (WMS) operations, fleet 
tracking updates, and ERP transact ion processing, resulting in an estimated 
revenue impact of INR 3.8 Crore per month.  
Single -Link 
Dependency  Hub warehouse sites (Tier -1) currently operate on a single MPLS link with no 
active failover path. Loss of connectivity at a hub site cascades to 8 –15 dependent 
spoke sites reliant on the hub for WMS access and IoT aggregation.  
No Cloud On -Ramp  Apex is executing a cloud -first transformation programme (ERP on Microsoft 
Azure, data analytics on AWS). Currently, all cloud traffic is backhauled through 
the Mumbai Data Centre, introducing latency of 180 –340 ms for branch -to-cloud 
workloads and consumi ng disproportionate Data Centre bandwidth.  
Security Perimeter 
Gaps  Branch sites lack next -generation firewall (NGFW) protection. A significant 
ransomware incident in Q3 FY2024 resulted in operational downtime of 72 hours 
across 14 warehouse sites and a forensic investigation cost of INR 1.1 Crore. Apex 
currently has no 24 /7 Security Operations Centre (SOC) capability.  
IoT Connectivity 
Fragmentation  Apex operates a fleet of 2.5 million IoT -enabled tracking SIMs across its vehicle 
fleet and warehouse assets. SIM management is currently distributed across three 
different Mobile Virtual Network Operators (MVNOs), resulting in fragmented 
visibility, incon sistent data plans, and inability to enforce unified MDM policies.  
1.3  Programme Objectives  
Apex Industrial Logistics Limited hereby issues this Request for Proposal to solicit proposals from qualified 
Managed Service Providers and System Integrators for the complete overhaul of its enterprise network, cloud 
connectivity, and security infrastruct ure. The primary objectives of this programme are:  
• Decommission all legacy MPLS circuits across 300 branch sites and replace with a fully managed 
SD-WAN overlay with active -active dual -link connectivity at hub sites.  
• Establish direct, carrier -grade Cloud Interconnects to Microsoft Azure (primary) and Amazon Web 
Services (secondary) from the Mumbai Data Centre, eliminating cloud backhaul latency.

3.1  Programme Description  
The Apex Network Transformation Programme (hereinafter "the Programme") is a strategic, enterprise -wide 
initiative to replace the Company's legacy MPLS network infrastructure with a modern, software -defined, cloud -
native managed network and security platfo rm. The Programme encompasses four primary workstreams 
executed in parallel under a unified programme governance structure:  
• Workstream 1 — Managed SD -WAN Deployment: Full replacement of MPLS circuits across all 300 
branch and hub sites with a managed SD -WAN overlay, incorporating active -active dual -link 
configuration at Tier -1 hub sites.  
• Workstream 2 — Cloud Direct Interconnect: Establishment of dedicated, carrier -grade private 
interconnects from Apex's Mumbai Data Centre directly to Microsoft Azure (ExpressRoute 
equivalent) and Amazon Web Services (AWS Direct Connect equivalent) at 10 Gbp s each.  
• Workstream 3 — Managed Security Operations Centre: Deployment and continuous 24x7x365 
monitoring of next -generation firewalls at all 300 branch edges by a certified SOC, including threat 
intelligence, SIEM correlation, and SLA -backed incident response.  
• Workstream 4 — Enterprise IoT Mobility Platform: Consolidation of 2.5 million fleet and asset 
tracking SIMs onto a single enterprise mobility management platform with unified dashboard, eSIM 
capability, and enforced MDM policy.

4.3  Workstream 3: 24/7 Managed Security Operations Centre (SOC)  
Apex requires a fully outsourced, 24x7x365 Managed SOC service to monitor, detect, investigate, and respond 
to cybersecurity threats across its entire network estate.  
 
4.3.1  SOC Service Scope  
• Continuous monitoring of all 300 SD -WAN edge NGFW devices for security events, policy violations, 
and anomalous traffic patterns.  
• Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) — Vendor must operate a cloud -delivered 
SIEM platform (e.g., Microsoft Sentinel, Splunk, IBM QRadar, or equivalent) ingesting logs from all 
edge firewalls, the Mumbai DC, cloud interconnects, and endpoint de tection agents.  
• Threat Intelligence integration: Vendor must subscribe to at least two (2) commercial Threat 
Intelligence feeds (e.g., Recorded Future, CrowdStrike Intelligence, Mandiant) and correlate IOCs 
against Apex's network traffic in near -real-time.  
• Vulnerability Management: Monthly automated VAPT scans across all in -scope network devices and 
quarterly manual penetration testing of the network perimeter, with findings reported to Apex CISO 
within 5 business days.  
• Incident Response: The vendor must provide Tier -1, Tier -2, and Tier -3 analyst capability within the 
SOC, with defined escalation paths. P1 incidents must be acknowledged within 15 minutes and 
contained within 4 hours. P1 is defined as any confirmed active threat affecting more than 5 sites or 
the Mumbai DC.  
• Root Cause Analysis (RCA): For all P1 and P2 security incidents, a formal RCA report must be 
delivered to Apex within 72 hours of incident containment, conforming to ITIL v4 Problem 
Management process.  
• Monthly SOC Operations Report: Delivered by the 5th business day of the following month, 
containing: event volumes, MTTD, MTTR, top threats, false -positive rate, and open vulnerability 
status.

stored exclusively within India -based data centres (Tier III or above). Storage outside India is 
prohibited without explicit written authorisation from Apex CI SO, consistent with DPDP Act 2023 
requirements.  
• The vendor must provide the name, location, and third -party audit certification of the data centre(s) 
used for SIEM log storage as part of the proposal.

11.3  Liability

any 12 -month period shall not exceed the total charges paid by Apex to the vendor in the preceding 
twelve (12) months.  
• Data Breach and Catastrophic Failure: Notwithstanding the general liability cap, in the event of: (a) a 
confirmed data breach attributable to the vendor's negligence or wilful misconduct resulting in the 
exfiltration of personal data of more than 10,000 Ap ex data subjects; or (b) a complete network 
outage affecting more than 50 Tier -2 or Tier -3 sites simultaneously for a period exceeding 8 
continuous hours; the vendor shall be subject to unlimited liability with no financial cap.  
• Consequential Loss: Subject to the unlimited liability carve -out above, neither party shall be liable to

DELIBERATE TRAP CLAUSE — UNLIMITED LIABILITY: The 'unlimited liability' provision for data

gross negligence and wilful misconduct, and cyber liability insurance coverage minimums 
consistent with SEBI CSCRF requirements.
